## Core Notes

### Ping, listen for the echo, time it

The HC-SR04 ranges the way bats and sonar do: **send out a burst of ultrasound, wait for it to bounce off an obstacle and return, and measure how long the round trip took**. Sound travels through air at a nearly constant speed (~**343 m/s**, i.e. 0.0343 cm/µs), so knowing the time gives you the distance.

Because the sound covers the distance twice (out and back):

> **distance = speed of sound × echo time ÷ 2**

### Trigger and echo: a two-pin conversation

The module has four pins: `VCC`, `GND`, `Trig` (trigger), `Echo`. One measurement runs like this:

1. **Trigger:** the MCU sends a **10 µs** HIGH pulse on `Trig`, telling the module to "ping".
2. **Ping:** the module automatically emits 8 pulses of 40 kHz ultrasound.
3. **Echo:** the module pulls `Echo` HIGH, and the HIGH lasts **exactly** as long as the sound's round trip; once the echo returns it goes LOW.

So all we have to do is measure how many microseconds `Echo` stayed HIGH.

### pulseIn and the distance formula

Arduino's `pulseIn` measures a pulse width in µs — exactly what we need:

```cpp
const int trig = 9, echo = 10;

void setup() {
  pinMode(trig, OUTPUT);
  pinMode(echo, INPUT);
  Serial.begin(9600);
}

void loop() {
  // 10 µs trigger pulse
  digitalWrite(trig, LOW);  delayMicroseconds(2);
  digitalWrite(trig, HIGH); delayMicroseconds(10);
  digitalWrite(trig, LOW);

  long us = pulseIn(echo, HIGH);       // echo width in microseconds
  float cm = us * 0.0343 / 2.0;        // convert to centimetres

  Serial.print(cm); Serial.println(" cm");
  delay(100);
}
```

In the interactive figure, drag the distance and watch the echo time grow linearly with it — 50 cm ≈ 2915 µs.

- **Blind zone:** too close (< 2 cm) and the echo returns before it can be timed, so the reading is off.
- **Angle:** the beam is a ~15° cone; against angled or sound-absorbing surfaces (cloth, foam) the echo is weak and may be missed.
- **Temperature:** the speed of sound varies with temperature; correct for it when you need accuracy.
- **Leave a gap between readings** (≥ 60 ms) so the previous echo dies away, or measurements interfere.

> ✦ **Key point:** it's an acoustic tape measure. The whole trick: trigger once → time the echo with `pulseIn` → multiply by the speed of sound and divide by 2. Grasp "time difference = distance" and you understand a whole family of sensors, from parking radar to laser range-finders.
